General Sir John Monash

Soldier, Scholar and Engineer

1880
Graduates Dux (equal) at Scotch College
1882
Begins studying Engineering at Melbourne University
1893
Graduates from the University of Melbourne: a Master of Engineering
1895
Graduates from Melbourne University: a Bachelor of Arts and Bachelor of Law
1921
Obtains a Doctor of Engineering
1922
Founding member of the Rotary Club of Melbourne, Australia’s first Rotary Club
1923
Appointed Vice~Chancellor of the University of Melbourne
1958
Monash University inaugurated

"Adopt as your fundamental creed that you will equip yourself for life, not solely for your own benefit but for the benefit of the whole community."

Sir John Monash
1884
Joins the university company of the militia
1887
Made Lieutenant in the North Melbourne battery militia unit
1897
Made Major of the North Melbourne battery
1906
Made Lieutenant-Colonel in the intelligence corps
1908
Commands the Victorian section of the Australian Intelligence Corps
1913
Made Colonel commanding the 13th Infantry Brigade
1915
Goes to War, appointed commander 4th Infantry Brigade, AIF, Gallipoli
1916
Made Major-General, commanding 3rd Australian Division in France
1917
Monash is involved in many battles including Messines, Broodseinde, and the First Battle of Passchendaele. The British High Command is impressed by Monash’s abilities and enthusiasm
1918
Monash is promoted to lieutenant general and made commander of the Australian Corps
1918
Knighted in the field
1919
Leads Australian troops in London victory march on 19 July 1919
1927
Monash uses the ANZAC Day march to garner support for the Shrine of Remembrance

"I would name Sir John Monash as the best general on the western front in Europe."

British Field Marshal Bernard Montgomery
1885
Begins working for David Munro who operates the largest engineering workshop in Australia
1888
Oversees construction of Melbourne’s Outer Circle Railway Line
1893
Works as a civil engineer, playing a major role in introducing reinforced concrete to Australian engineering practice
1894
Enters into partnership with JT Anderson as consultants and contractors
1905
Forms Reinforced Concrete and Monier Pipe Construction Co Ltd
1920
Monash is appointed General Manager, State Electricity Commission

"The creativity, loyalty and affection [Monash] inspired seem to have few or no parallels in any other large Australian corporation. [He was] a champion who saw the SEC through to an unusual position of public pride and acclaim for a State instrumentality."
